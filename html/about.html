HI, I'M JOEY!

I'm currently a junior year computer science student at Texas A&M University at College Station. I like web design and front-end software development.

The "painting" to the right (or bottom, depending on the device you're using to view this website) is a rough depiction of what I look like.

TECH SKILLS

I have experience in C++ (2+ years), Python (4 years), and Java (5 years) for general programming languages, HTML and CSS for markup, and JavaScript as a supplementary language for web development (all 1+ years). I've also worked in R Studio, Scheme (a variation of Lisp), as well as dabbled in machine and assembly language via the Nand2Tetris platform.

To code, I use Atom, Visual Studio Code, Eclipse, NetBeans, PyCharm, and DrRacket. I'm also trying to get into XCode for app development.

OTHER SKILLS

Other tools I use include Adobe XD for prototyping website designs, Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator for editing and creating visual artwork (I made all the visuals on this website!), and FL Studio and Ableton for music production.

I also have an interdisciplinary focus in Russian language and culture (Привет, мои русские друзья!).

MY INTERESTS

Outside of school, I enjoy listening to and making music (here's my Spotify), watching Netflix and other TV shows and movies (if it wasn't obvious enough, I'm very fond of Wes Anderson), reading (currently reading Infinite Jest and The Pale King by David Foster Wallace), and running my radio show on KANM's student radio (which you can listen to here on Fridays at 2 PM!).

MY PHILOSOPHY

I've always had a strong appreciation for and interest in all forms of art, including and especially the visual arts. From my experiences with studying both classic and modern aesthetics, I've come to embrace simplicity in content structure and design patterns, applying this to my field by emphasizing cleanliness and efficiency in how my code appears to others. I hope to, one day, fully bring my enthusiasm and passion for sleek and creative visual design into my work as a programmer.
